Welcome to Mars






Tourist spots:

  • Olympus Mons is the largest volcano in the solar system, with an elevation of about 22 kilometers (13.6 miles) above the Martian surface.
  • It is a shield volcano, characterized by its broad, gently sloping profile.
  • Located in the Tharsis volcanic plateau, Olympus Mons has a massive summit caldera with collapse pits and fissures.
  • The volcano's relatively young age suggests relatively recent volcanic activity.

  • Arabia Terra is a large upland region on Mars characterized by its heavily cratered surface.
  • It is one of the oldest terrains on the planet, with some features dating back billions of years.
  • The region contains a variety of geological features, including highland plateaus, valleys, and ancient river channels

  • Syrtis Major is a dark, lowland region on Mars known for its volcanic plains
  • It is one of the most prominent dark albedo features on the planet and has been associated with ancient volcanic activity
  • The region contains both large shield volcanoes and extensive lava flows

  • Elysium is a volcanic region on Mars with a group of three large shield volcanoes, including Elysium Mons
  • The largest volcano in this group, Elysium Mons, is about 12 kilometers (7.5 miles) high
  • The region is relatively young in geological terms and has evidence of recent volcanic activity

  • Arcadia Planitia is a plain in the northern lowlands of Mars, known for its relatively flat terrain
  • It contains a number of impact craters and is characterized by its diverse geological features
